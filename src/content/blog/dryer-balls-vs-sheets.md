### Analyzing the "Chemical Subscription Model"
Standard commercial dryer sheets operate on a recurring cost basis. They achieve softness by deposition: coating textiles in a thin layer of melted stearic acid (derived wax). This method, while functional, introduces long-term inefficiencies:
* **Decreased Absorbency:** The wax barrier drastically reduces the hydroscopic performance of terry cloth (towels) over successive cycles.
* **Operational Strain:** Residual buildup on ventilation and lint screens can restrict airflow, increasing energy demand and fire safety risks.
* **Recursive Cost:** Households are essentially paying an annual premium for a product that induces systemic inefficiencies.

### Mechanics of Wool Dryer Ball Performance (Energy Optimization)
Wool alternatives rely on mechanical kinetic energy rather than chemical transfer. During operation, high-density balls maintain separation within the load:
1. **Improved Airflow:** They create necessary void spaces, allowing thermal energy to saturate the textiles uniformly.
2. **Moisture Extraction:** Wool naturally accelerates the hydration phase change, absorbing ambient moisture and reducing necessary cycle duration.
