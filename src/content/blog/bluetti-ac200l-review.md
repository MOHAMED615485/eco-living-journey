## 👎 What I Do Not Like

**1. Weight**
28kg is heavy. I need two hands and full attention to move it. Compared to the 11.8kg Jackery, this is a different category of portability. If you need to move your generator between rooms regularly, this will frustrate you.

**2. Price**
The AC200L costs significantly more than the EcoFlow or Jackery. That premium is justified by the performance but it is a real barrier.

**3. Fan noise at high output**
Above 1,500W continuous draw the fan becomes audible. Not loud, but noticeable in a quiet room. For my garage setup this was irrelevant. For a bedroom setup it might matter.

**4. Size**
This is a substantial unit. It needs dedicated space. It does not tuck under a desk or into a small closet.

## ❓ FAQ

**How long does the Bluetti AC200L last on one charge?**
Running a chest freezer and refrigerator simultaneously at 295W average draw, I got 11.2 hours per charge. Running just a chest freezer at 95W average, I got 18.4 hours. Your runtime depends entirely on your total watt load.

**Can the Bluetti AC200L power a whole house?**
No portable unit can power a whole house. The AC200L covers essential appliances — chest freezer, refrigerator, lights, router, phones — for 10-12 hours on a single charge. For true whole-home backup you need a permanently installed system like a Tesla Powerwall.

**Is the Bluetti AC200L worth the extra cost over EcoFlow?**
Only if you need to run multiple appliances simultaneously. For a single chest freezer or refrigerator, the EcoFlow DELTA 3 Plus delivers better value. The AC200L earns its premium when you need to run everything at once for extended periods.

**How many solar panels do I need to charge the Bluetti AC200L?**
Two 350W panels will charge it from zero in about 5-6 hours in direct sun. Four 300W panels cuts that to under 2 hours. The 1,200W solar input capacity means you can throw more panels at it and it keeps up.
