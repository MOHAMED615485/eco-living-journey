## The Number That Decides Everything

Before I get into the real-world testing, let me explain the one number that makes this comparison matter more than any spec sheet suggests.

Your chest freezer does not pull a steady load. It cycles. The compressor kicks on from a dead stop every 30–45 minutes and pulls a massive surge of electricity in the fraction of a second before the motor gets spinning. This is called the **Locked Rotor Amps** — LRA.

On my 7 cu. ft. Midea chest freezer:
- **Running watts:** 172W
- **Surge watts (LRA × 120):** 954W

Both units handle 954W of surge easily. So for my specific freezer, both would work.

But here is where it gets interesting. If you have a larger freezer — say a 15 cu. ft. unit with an LRA of 12.5 — your surge watts are 1,500W. The Jackery's 2,000W surge handles that with 500W of headroom. Still fine.

Now add a refrigerator. Or a well pump. Or try to run both the freezer AND the fridge simultaneously during a blackout. Suddenly the Jackery's 2,000W surge ceiling becomes a real constraint. The EcoFlow's 7,200W is so far above any realistic residential surge that it simply never becomes the problem.

That margin — 7,200W vs 2,000W — is the entire story of this comparison.

## 💡 Real World Testing: What Actually Happened

### The Summer Thunderstorm Test — August

14-hour outage. Garage temperature: 94°F. Freezer 55% full.

**EcoFlow DELTA 3 Plus:**
Running simultaneous load: chest freezer + laptop + Wi-Fi + 3 LED bulbs = ~340W continuous.
Compressor surges: peaked at 970W every cycle.
Result: **62% charge remaining** after 14 hours.

**Jackery Explorer 1000 V2:**
Same load, same conditions, tested two weeks later during a different outage.
Compressor surges: same 970W peaks — Jackery handled them without issue.
Result: **44% charge remaining** after 14 hours.

Observation: The Jackery used more battery percentage for the same load. This is partly because its continuous output works harder near its ceiling, and partly because the 1,070Wh vs 1,024Wh difference is smaller than you would expect in practice.

### The November Grid Failure — 22 Hours

Utility crew hit a transformer. 22 hours without power. Garage at 54°F. Freezer packed full with frozen water bottles.

**EcoFlow DELTA 3 Plus:**
Result: **41% charge remaining** after 22 hours. Food perfectly frozen.

**Jackery Explorer 1000 V2:**
I did not run the Jackery for this test — I was not going to risk 22 hours of food on the unit I was less confident in. That decision tells you something about how I actually feel about the two products.

## 🛠️ How to Choose: The 3-Question Framework

**Question 1: What is your freezer's LRA?**
Pull the data plate from the back of your freezer. Find the LRA number. Multiply by 120. If the result is under 1,400W, both units handle it. If it is over 1,400W, go with the EcoFlow.

**Question 2: Will you run multiple appliances simultaneously?**
If you plan to run just a freezer and maybe some lights and a phone charger — the Jackery is sufficient. If you want to run a freezer AND a refrigerator AND a Wi-Fi router AND charge devices — the EcoFlow's higher continuous output and surge capacity gives you genuine comfort.

**Question 3: How important is charging speed?**
If a storm warning gives you 2 hours of notice, the EcoFlow goes from empty to 80% in that time. The Jackery does not. If you keep your battery topped up permanently, this matters less. If you are like most people and think about it only when a storm is coming — the EcoFlow's fast charging is worth real money.
